Tiwa Eisape

I am a third-year PhD student ay MIT in the Department of Brain and Cognitive Sciences and the Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Lab. I'm co-advised by Roger Levy and Yoon Kim.

I use methods from machine learning to study and model human language comprehension. My work is supported by an NSF GRFP, a GEM Full fellowship, and an MIT Dean of Sciences fellowship. I also organize complang a speaker series/reading group on language and computation at MIT.

Eisape T., Zaslavsky N, & Levy R.
Conference on Computational Natural Language Learning, 2020

We evaluate language models' ability to predict human next-word predictions and reading times and propose Cloze Distillationa method for distilling the information from cloze data into pre-trained LMs.
